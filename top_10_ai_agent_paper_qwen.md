
As of 2025, agentic AI—referring to AI systems that can act autonomously, reason over goals, plan, interact with environments, and often collaborate or compete with other agents—has become a rapidly evolving subfield of artificial intelligence. While there is no universally agreed-upon ranking, the following 10 papers are widely recognized as foundational or highly influential in shaping the theory, architecture, and applications of agentic AI. These selections are based on citation impact, community reception (e.g., at top AI conferences like NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, AAAI), and their role in advancing key capabilities such as planning, tool use, memory, self-reflection, and multi-agent coordination.

---

### **Top 10 Papers in Agentic AI (as of 2025)**

1. **"ReAct: Synergizing Reasoning and Acting in Language Models"**
   *Shunyu Yao, Jeffrey Zhao, Dian Yu, Nan Du, Izhak Shafran, Karthik Narasimhan, Yuan Cao* (ICLR 2023)
   - Introduced the ReAct framework, combining reasoning (chain-of-thought) with action (tool/environment interaction) in a unified loop.
   - Highly influential for enabling LLMs to act as goal-driven agents.

2. **"Generative Agents: Interactive Simulacra of Human Behavior"**
   *Joon Sung Park, Joseph C. O’Brien, Carrie J. Cai, Meredith Ringel Morris, Percy Liang, Michael S. Bernstein* (ACM CHI 2023)
   - Demonstrated agents with memory, planning, and social interaction in a simulated town (Smallville).
   - Pioneered architectures for persistent, believable agent behavior.

3. **"Toolformer: Language Models Can Teach Themselves to Use Tools"**
   *Timo Schick, Jane Dwivedi-Yu, Roberto Dessì, Roberta Raileanu, Maria Lomeli, Luke Zettlemoyer, Nicolas Usunier, Thomas Scialom* (NeurIPS 2023)
   - Showed that LLMs can be trained to decide when and how to call external APIs (e.g., calculators, search).
   - Laid groundwork for tool-augmented agentic systems.

4. **"Reflexion: Language Agents with Verbal Reinforcement Learning"**
   *Noah Shinn, Federico Cassano, Akhil Gopinath, Karthik Narasimhan, Shunyu Yao* (NeurIPS 2023)
   - Introduced self-reflection as a form of verbal reinforcement learning, enabling agents to improve via trial-and-error with natural language feedback.
   - Key for iterative self-improvement in agentic workflows.

5. **"Agent57: Outperforming the Atari Human Benchmark"**
   *Adrià Puigdomènech Badia, Bilal Piot, Steven Kapturowski, Pablo Sprechmann, Oleksandr Vinyals, Demis Hassabis, Charles Blundell* (ICML 2020)
   - Though earlier, this DeepMind paper was pivotal in showing a single agent could master all 57 Atari games, introducing meta-learning and intrinsic motivation.
   - Influential in reinforcement learning–based agentic design.

6. **"CAMEL: Communicative Agents for ‘Mind’ Exploration of Large Scale Language Model Society"**
   *Guohao Li, Hasan Abed Al Kader Hammoud, Hani Itani, Dmitrii Khizbullin, Bernard Ghanem* (NeurIPS 2023 Workshop)
   - Explored role-playing multi-agent societies where agents collaborate to solve tasks via communication.
   - Highlighted emergent behaviors and division of labor in LLM-based agent teams.

7. **"MetaGPT: Meta Programming for Multi-Agent Collaborative Framework"**
   *Yuxiang Huang, Zhenchao Li, Ao Liu, Weiran Yao, Shengyu Zhang, Yongfeng Zhang* (arXiv 2023)
   - Proposed a multi-agent system that mimics software company workflows (product manager, engineer, etc.).
   - Demonstrated structured coordination and role specialization among agents.

8. **"AutoGPT: An Autonomous GPT-4 Experiment"**
   *Toran Bruce Richards* (GitHub, 2023 — not a formal paper, but highly influential)
   - While not peer-reviewed, AutoGPT catalyzed widespread interest in autonomous LLM agents capable of chaining goals, using memory, and executing tools.
   - Inspired numerous academic and open-source agentic frameworks.

9. **"Language Agents as Embodied AI: A Survey and Roadmap"**
   *Yao Fu, Hao Peng, Ashwin Ram, et al.* (arXiv 2024)
   - A comprehensive survey that formalized the agentic AI paradigm, proposing taxonomies for reasoning, memory, tool use, and social interaction.
   - Helped unify terminology and research directions in the field.

10. **"SWE-Agent: An LLM Agent that Can Fix Real-World GitHub Issues"**
   *John Yang, Zhaoyuan Sun, et al.* (ICLR 2025)
   - Demonstrated a specialized agentic system that autonomously debugs and patches real open-source code repositories.
   - Showed high practical utility and set new benchmarks for task-oriented agentic performance.

---

### Honorable Mentions:
- **"Voyager: An Open-Ended Embodied Agent with Large Language Models"** (NeurIPS 2023) – for Minecraft-based lifelong learning agents.
- **"GPT-4V(ision) as a Generalist Agent"** (Microsoft, 2023) – explored multimodal agentic capabilities.
- **"OpenAGI: When LLM Meets Domain Experts"** (ICLR 2024) – framework for routing tasks to specialized modules.

---

These papers collectively define the core capabilities of modern agentic AI: **autonomy, reasoning, memory, tool use, self-improvement, and social coordination**. The field continues to evolve rapidly, with new architectures emerging monthly—but the above represent key milestones as of late 2025.
